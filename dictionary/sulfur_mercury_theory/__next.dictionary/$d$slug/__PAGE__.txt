3:T417,<p>The sulfur-mercury theory holds that all metals are composed of two philosophical principles: sulfur (active, hot, masculine, solar) and mercury (passive, cold, feminine, lunar). Metals differ only in proportion and purity of these constituents; transmutation requires purifying and recombining them in ideal balance to produce gold.</p>
<p>Hortulanus&#39;s fourteenth-century commentary decoded the Emerald Tablet through this lens: the Sun as philosophical sulfur, the Moon as mercury, wind as vaporous circulation within the sealed vessel, earth as fixation through solid ferment. This reading transformed the Tablet&#39;s cosmological pronouncements into a precise laboratory recipe.</p>
<p>The theory bridges Hermetic cosmology and practice by mapping macrocosm-microcosm correspondence onto specific operations: just as the cosmos was generated from active and passive cosmic principles, so the Philosopher&#39;s Stone is generated from purified sulfur and mercury. Newton continued this interpretive tradition in his own commentary.</p>
