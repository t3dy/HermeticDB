<p style="font-style:italic;color:#6b5d4d">كتاب سر الخليقة وصنعة الطبيعة</p>
<p>The Kitab Sirr al-Khaliqa wa San'at al-Tabi'a (Book of the Secret of Creation and the Art of Nature) is an expansive Arabic cosmological encyclopedia attributed to Balinas (the Arabic name for Apollonius of Tyana), also known as the Kitab al-'Ilal (Book of Causes). The text was likely composed in the late sixth to eighth century CE, drawing on Greek, Syriac, and possibly Persian sources.</p><p>The work is structured into six books following the sequence of creation: God, celestial spheres, minerals, plants, animals, and humans. It presents a comprehensive natural philosophy that synthesizes Aristotelian physics, Neoplatonic emanation theory, and alchemical doctrine within a pseudepigraphical frame narrative. Most critically, the Sirr al-Khaliqa contains the earliest known contextual embedding of the Emerald Tablet, presented as a text discovered by Balinas in a subterranean vault beneath a statue of Hermes. Two recensions exist: a shorter version (A) closer to the presumed Greek original, and an expanded version (B). The longer recension incorporates material from Nemesius of Emesa's De Natura Hominis, demonstrating active synthesis of Neoplatonic anthropology with alchemical theory.</p><p>Ursula Weisser's 1979 critical edition, based on seventeen manuscripts, established the definitive Arabic text. Julius Ruska's 1926 monograph on the Tabula Smaragdina first demonstrated that the Sirr al-Khaliqa represents the original literary context of the Emerald Tablet. Over thirty manuscripts survive across major libraries.</p><p>The Sirr al-Khaliqa is indispensable for understanding the Emerald Tablet's origins and the broader process by which Greek philosophical and alchemical ideas were transmitted into the Arabic world and subsequently into medieval Latin Europe through translations such as Hugo of Santalla's De secretis naturae.</p>
<h3 style="margin-top:1.5rem;font-size:1.1rem">Transmission</h3>
<p>Also known as Kitab Balaniyus al-Hakim fi'l-Ilal (Book of Wise Balinas on the Causes). Over 30 manuscripts across major libraries. Weisser (1979) critical edition based on 17 manuscripts.</p>
