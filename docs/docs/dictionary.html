Hermetic Dictionary

Encyclopedic index of 19 philosophical, alchemical, and theological concepts.

Anthropos (Primal Human)
PHILOSOPHICAL

Original archetypal human containing the microcosm of all creation; human nature as essentially divine.

Barbarous Names (Onomata Barbara)
LINGUISTIC

Foreign, non-Greek sacred names carrying inherent divine energy that cannot survive translation.

Decknamen (Cover Names)
LINGUISTIC

The systematic use of symbolic cover names in alchemical texts, where a single term simultaneously refers to celestial bodies, metals, and operative stages.

Demiurge (Craftsman God)
COSMOLOGICAL

Second-order creative deity responsible for shaping the material world.

Discovery Topos (Hidden Chamber Narrative)
PHILOSOPHICAL

The late antique literary motif of discovering ancient wisdom in a hidden underground chamber, used to authenticate esoteric knowledge.

Emanation (Exitus a Deo)
THEOLOGICAL

The Neoplatonic doctrine that material multiplicity cascades from a singular, indivisible source.

Four Elemental Qualities
PHILOSOPHICAL

The four primary qualities (hot, cold, moist, dry) that combine to form the four elements and drive all material transformations.

Gnosis (Saving Knowledge)
THEOLOGICAL

Experiential knowledge of divine nature and cosmic structure; transformative recognition of self as divine.

Logos (Divine Word)
PHILOSOPHICAL

Rational principle of creation; divine utterance that structures reality; intermediary between transcendent One and material cosmos.

Macrocosm and Microcosm
COSMOLOGICAL

The principle that the structure of the human being (microcosm) mirrors the structure of the universe (macrocosm).

Magnum Opus (Great Work)
ALCHEMICAL

The alchemical process of creating the Philosopher's Stone through stages of transformation (nigredo, albedo, citrinitas, rubedo).

Nous (Divine Mind)
PHILOSOPHICAL

The supreme intellect; first principle of being below the One; consciousness as cosmic principle.

Palingenesia (Regeneration)
THEOLOGICAL

Spiritual rebirth through shedding material identification and assuming divine consciousness.

Prisca Theologia (Ancient Theology)
PHILOSOPHICAL

The Renaissance doctrine that a single divine truth was given to humanity in antiquity and passed through a chain of sages including Hermes, Moses, Orpheus, Pythagoras, and Plato.

Sulfur-Mercury Theory
ALCHEMICAL

The doctrine that all metals are composed of philosophical sulfur (active masculine principle) and philosophical mercury (passive feminine principle).

Sympatheia (Universal Correspondence)
COSMOLOGICAL

Universal resonance and correspondence between all levels of reality; the ability of lower to affect upper through right knowledge.

Telesmi / Telesmum
LINGUISTIC

Transliterated Arabic term (abu al-tilasmat, 'father of talismans') that entered Latin untranslated and became one of the most disputed words in alchemical exegesis.

Theurgy (Divine Work)
THEOLOGICAL

Sacred ritual designed to enact union with the divine through participatory action rather than coercive manipulation.

VITRIOL Acrostic
ALCHEMICAL

The Latin acrostic 'Visita Interiora Terrae Rectificando Invenies Occultum Lapidem' (Visit the interior of the earth and by rectifying you will find the hidden stone).
