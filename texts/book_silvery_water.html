Book of the Silvery Water and the Starry Earth

Unknown Era · ARABIC

ARABIC · TREATISE · 900–960 CE

The Kitab al-Ma' al-Waraqi wa'l-Ard al-Najmiya (Book of the Silvery Water and the Starry Earth) is an Arabic alchemical treatise composed by Muhammad ibn Umayl al-Sadiq al-Tamimi (ca. 900-960 CE), one of the most important alchemists of the tenth-century Islamic world. The work later circulated in Latin translation as the Tabula Chemica, with its author known in Western sources as Senior Zadith.

The treatise presents alchemical theory through richly symbolic and allegorical language, employing imagery of celestial correspondences and material transformation. Of particular significance for the history of the Emerald Tablet, Ibn Umayl's text contains a distinct variant of the discovery narrative in which an alchemical stone table rests across the knees of Hermes's corpse inside an Egyptian pyramid, inscribed with hieroglyphic writing described as birbawi (priestly script). This version differs markedly from the Sirr al-Khaliqa's account, suggesting independent transmission lines for the Tablet legend.

The Book of the Silvery Water is important for understanding the diversity of Hermetic textual traditions in the Arabic world. Its unique variant of the Emerald Tablet discovery narrative links the Tablet directly to Egyptian pyramid traditions and priestly writing, reinforcing the Egyptian identity of Hermes Trismegistus as a transmitter of ancient wisdom. The work demonstrates that by the tenth century, the Emerald Tablet had acquired multiple competing origin stories, each serving to authenticate its authority within different alchemical lineages.
