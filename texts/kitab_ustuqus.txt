<p style="font-style:italic;color:#6b5d4d">كتاب أسطقس الأس الثاني</p>
<p>The Kitab Ustuqus al-Uss al-Thani (Second Book of the Elements of Foundation) is an Arabic alchemical treatise attributed to Jabir ibn Hayyan, the legendary eighth-to-ninth-century alchemist whose vast corpus of writings (the Jabirian corpus) forms a cornerstone of Islamic alchemical literature. The Jabirian corpus is now generally understood as a collective pseudepigraphical effort, likely produced by scholars of possible Isma'ili persuasion over an extended period.</p><p>The text is of particular importance for the history of the Emerald Tablet because it contains an early Arabic version of the Tablet that was first identified by the British scholar Eric John Holmyard, who published it from a Bombay lithograph edition of 1891. Peter Zirnis later made the text the subject of a critical edition in 1979. The version of the Emerald Tablet found in the Kitab Ustuqus differs from the version in the Sirr al-Khaliqa, suggesting independent lines of transmission for the Tablet text within the Arabic alchemical tradition.</p><p>The treatise is significant for understanding the early circulation and variant forms of the Emerald Tablet in the Arabic world. Its attribution to Jabir ibn Hayyan places it within the most prolific and influential corpus of Islamic alchemical writing, demonstrating that the Tablet was considered authoritative across multiple schools of Arabic alchemy. The existence of this independent textual witness also complicates any simple linear account of the Tablet's transmission history.</p>
<h3 style="margin-top:1.5rem;font-size:1.1rem">Transmission</h3>
<p>Part of the Jabirian corpus. Holmyard was the first to find this version of the Emerald Tablet.</p>
