<p>The Discourse on the Eighth and Ninth is a Coptic Hermetic text preserved in Nag Hammadi Codex VI, discovered in Upper Egypt in 1945. The original was composed in Greek, likely between the second and fourth centuries CE, and translated into Coptic as part of the broader Nag Hammadi library. It is one of the most important Hermetic texts discovered in the twentieth century.</p><p>The text describes a visionary ascent through the cosmic spheres, specifically the eighth and ninth celestial realms beyond the seven planetary spheres of conventional ancient cosmology. The initiate, guided by Hermes, experiences ecstatic union with the divine through prayer, vocalic hymns (sequences of vowel sounds used as ritual utterances), and contemplative practice. The discourse presents Hermetic initiation as a progressive spiritual ascent culminating in direct experience of the divine Nous.</p><p>The Discourse on the Eighth and Ninth is particularly valuable because it reveals the initiatory and mystical dimensions of Hermeticism that are less explicit in the more philosophical treatises of the Corpus Hermeticum.
