<p>Sagijus of Nablus (also rendered as Sergius; dates uncertain) is a figure named in an introductory preface to one of the Arabic recensions of the Emerald Tablet, discovered by Julius Ruska in the early twentieth century. According to this twelfth-century recension, the Tablet was dictated by a Christian priest named Sagijus.</p><p>Scholars have suggested that Sagijus may preserve a historical memory of Sergius of Reshaina (d. 536 CE), a sixth-century Syriac physician and translator who rendered Greek philosophical and medical works into Syriac.
