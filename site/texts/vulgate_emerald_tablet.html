LATIN · TRANSLATION · 1134–1145 CE

Tabula Smaragdina

The most influential Latin version of the Emerald Tablet, likely translated by Plato of Tivoli in Barcelona. Strips away the narrative framing of the Sirr al-Khaliqa, leaving potent aphorisms. Foundational for commentaries by Albertus Magnus and Roger Bacon.

Transmission

Served as the base text for virtually all subsequent European engagement with the Tablet. Preserved in manuscripts like Arundel MS 164.
