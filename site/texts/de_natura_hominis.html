On the Nature of Man (De Natura Hominis) TREATISE

GREEK · TREATISE · 390–400 CE

Περὶ φύσεως ἀνθρώπου

Treatise by Nemesius of Emesa defining the human being as a microcosm binding celestial and terrestrial spheres. One of the first holistic anthropological treatises, heavily engaged with Middle Platonism and Stoicism. The concept of macrocosm-microcosm correspondence is the direct theoretical precursor to the Emerald Tablet's most famous axiom.

Transmission

An Arabic translation appears at the end of the Sirr al-Khaliqa, proving the compilers were actively synthesizing Nemesius's Neoplatonic anthropology with alchemical theory.
