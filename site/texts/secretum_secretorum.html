Secret of Secrets (Secretum Secretorum) COMPILATION

ARABIC · COMPILATION · 800–900 CE

كتاب سر الأسرار / Secretum Secretorum

Pseudo-Aristotelian compendium framed as letters from Aristotle to Alexander the Great on statecraft, morality, physiognomy, medicine, astrology, and alchemy. Contains the Emerald Tablet as integrated alchemical and political wisdom. One of the most widely read books of the European High Middle Ages.

Transmission

First translated to Latin by John of Seville c. 1140 (abridged), then fully by Philip of Tripoli c. 1243.
