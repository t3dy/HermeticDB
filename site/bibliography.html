Bibliography

Primary Sources 16

Brian P. Copenhaver (1992) EDITION In Collection

Hermetica: The Greek Corpus Hermeticum and the Latin Asclepius in a New English Translation with Notes and Introduction

Cambridge University Press

Standard English translation. Primary scholarly reference for philosophical Hermetica.

Christian H. Bull (2018) MONOGRAPH In Collection

The Tradition of Hermes Trismegistus: The Egyptian Priestly Figure as a Teacher of Hellenized Wisdom

Brill

548 pages. Argues for Egyptian priestly authorship and ritual dimensions.

Claudio Moreschini (2005) MONOGRAPH In Collection

Hermes Christianus: The Intermingling of Hermetic Piety and Christian Thought

Brepols

Hermetic-Christian theological fusion from late antiquity through Renaissance.

Didier Kahn (1994) MONOGRAPH

La table d'émeraude et sa tradition alchimique

Les Belles Lettres

Central modern monograph on the Tablet's textual and interpretive tradition.

Eric John Holmyard (1923) ARTICLE

The Emerald Table

Nature

Early scholarly discussion of the Emerald Tablet's Arabic context.

Florian Ebeling and Jan Assmann (2007) MONOGRAPH In Collection

The Secret History of Hermes Trismegistus: Hermeticism from Ancient to Modern Times

Cornell University Press

Diachronic reception history. Establishes periodization methodology.

Françoise Hudry (1997) ARTICLE

Le De secretis nature du ps.-Apollonius de Tyane, traduction latine par Hugues de Santalla du Kitâb sirr al-halîqa

Chrysopoeia

Key modern edition for Hugo of Santalla's Latin translation. Chrysopoeia 6 (1997-1999): 1-154.

Garth Fowden (1986) MONOGRAPH

The Egyptian Hermes: A Historical Approach to the Late Pagan Mind

Cambridge University Press

Foundational work establishing Egyptian priestly context.

Julius Ruska (1926) MONOGRAPH

Tabula Smaragdina: Ein Beitrag zur Geschichte der hermetischen Literatur

Carl Winter

Foundational modern philological study. Dated composition 600-750 CE.

Kyle Fraser (2025) ARTICLE In Collection

Hermetism and Hieratic Tradition in the De mysteriis of Iamblichus

Greek, Roman, and Byzantine Studies

Bridges Hermetic and Iamblichean studies through shared vocabulary (exitalos).

Paolo Lucentini, Ilaria Parri, and Vittoria Perrone Compagni (eds.) (2003) COLLECTION

Hermetism from Late Antiquity to Humanism

Brepols (IPM 40)

Proceedings of 2001 Naples Hermes Latinus conference. Key contributions on medieval transmission.

Paul Kraus (1942) MONOGRAPH

Jābir ibn Ḥayyān: Contribution à l'histoire des idées scientifiques dans l'Islam

Institut français d'archéologie orientale

Demonstrated collective authorship of Jabirian corpus. Analyzed Sirr al-Khaliqa sources and influence.

Robert Steele and Dorothea Waley Singer (1928) ARTICLE

The Emerald Table

Proceedings of the Royal Society of Medicine

Classic study and edition of the Latin tradition. Source of the well-known vulgate text.

Silvestre de Sacy (1799) ARTICLE

Le livre du secret de la créature

Demonstrated connection between Sirr al-Khaliqa and Apollonius of Tyana, correcting Pliny misidentification.

Ursula Weisser (1979) EDITION In Collection

Buch über das Geheimnis der Schöpfung und die Darstellung der Natur (Buch der Ursachen) von Pseudo-Apollonios von Tyana

Institute for the History of Arabic Science, University of Aleppo

Critical edition of Arabic Sirr al-Khaliqa. Based on collation of 17 manuscripts. Establishes Recension A and B.

Wouter J. Hanegraaff (2022) MONOGRAPH In Collection

Hermetic Spirituality and the Historical Imagination: Altered States of Knowledge in Late Antiquity

Cambridge University Press

Visionary and experiential dimensions of Hermeticism.

Direct Sources 8

Allen G. Debus (1977) MONOGRAPH In Collection

The Chemical Philosophy: Paracelsian Science and Medicine in the Sixteenth and Seventeenth Centuries

Science History Publications

759 pages. Traces Paracelsian-Hermetic influence on early modern science.

Bauer and Lindemann (TEAM LAXiTY) COLLECTION In Collection

History and Translations of Emerald Tablet

Collected translations with verse numbering for comparison. Our primary source for parallel translation data.

Betty Jo Teeter Dobbs (1988) MONOGRAPH

The Foundations of Newton's Alchemy

Cambridge University Press

Key work on Newton's alchemical studies.

Eric John Holmyard (1957) MONOGRAPH

Alchemy

Penguin Books

Standard history of alchemy. Holmyard's account of finding the Jabirian version.

M. David Litwa (2018) EDITION

Hermetica II: The Excerpts of Stobaeus, Papyrus Fragments, and Ancient Testimonies

Cambridge University Press

Modern synthesis with Emerald Tablet chapter, notes, and bibliography.

Peter J. Forshaw (2020) MONOGRAPH In Collection

The Mage's Images: Heinrich Khunrath in His Oratory and Laboratory

Brill

2 volumes (498+482 pages). Leading study of Khunrath's Hermetic-alchemical visual culture.

William R. Newman (2019) MONOGRAPH

Newton the Alchemist: Science, Enigma, and the Quest for Nature's Secret Fire

Princeton University Press

Useful for early modern and Newtonian reception of the Emerald Tablet.

Wouter J. Hanegraaff and Ruud M. Bouthoorn (2005) EDITION In Collection

Lodovico Lazzarelli (1447-1500): The Hermetic Writings and Related Documents

ACMRS

376 pages. Critical edition of Lazzarelli's Hermetic writings.

Contextual Sources 2

Joseph Needham (1980) MONOGRAPH

Science and Civilisation in China

Cambridge University Press

Suggested possible Chinese alchemical origins for the Emerald Tablet. Minority position.

Sarah A. Lang ARTICLE

A Machine Reasoning Algorithm for the Digital Analysis of Alchemical Language and its Decknamen

PubMed / Taylor & Francis

Methodology for digital analysis of alchemical polyvalent terminology.
