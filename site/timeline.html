Timeline

Key events in the history of the Hermetic tradition.

100s CE

COMPOSITION Corpus Hermeticum treatises composed

Individual treatises of the philosophical Hermetica composed in Greco-Egyptian milieu.

Corpus Hermeticum · 100–300
300s CE

COMPOSITION Iamblichus writes De mysteriis

Defense of theurgy and Hermetic ritual practice against Porphyry's rationalism.

Iamblichus · De mysteriis (On the Mysteries) · 300

COMPOSITION Nemesius of Emesa writes De Natura Hominis

Defines the human being as a microcosm binding celestial and terrestrial spheres. Direct theoretical precursor to the Emerald Tablet's central axiom.

Nemesius of Emesa · On the Nature of Man (De Natura Hominis) · 390
600s CE

COMPOSITION Sirr al-Khaliqa composed/compiled

The cosmological encyclopedia attributed to Balinas is written, containing the earliest known contextual embedding of the Emerald Tablet.

Balinas (Pseudo-Apollonius of Tyana) · Book of the Secret of Creation (Kitab Sirr al-Khaliqa) · 650–833
800s CE

COMPOSITION Secretum Secretorum compiled

Pseudo-Aristotelian compendium compiled, embedding the Emerald Tablet within political-alchemical wisdom addressed to Alexander the Great.

Secret of Secrets (Secretum Secretorum) · 800

COMPOSITION Jabirian Emerald Tablet version produced

The Emerald Tablet appears in the Kitab Ustuqus al-Uss al-Thani, attributed to Jabir ibn Hayyan.

Jabir ibn Hayyan · Second Book of the Elements of Foundation · 850–950
900s CE

COMPOSITION Ibn Umayl's Emerald Tablet version

Ibn Umayl preserves a distinct discovery narrative placing the Tablet in an Egyptian pyramid.

Ibn Umayl · Book of the Silvery Water and the Starry Earth · 900
1100s CE

TRANSLATION John of Seville translates abridged Secretum Secretorum

First Latin translation of the pseudo-Aristotelian Secretum Secretorum, focused on medical and astrological content.

John of Seville · Secret of Secrets (Secretum Secretorum) · 1140

TRANSLATION Vulgate Latin Emerald Tablet produced

Plato of Tivoli (probably) produces the standalone Latin Emerald Tablet in Barcelona. This becomes the dominant European version.

Plato of Tivoli · Vulgate Latin Emerald Tablet · 1140

TRANSLATION Hugo of Santalla translates Sirr al-Khaliqa

First complete Latin translation of the Kitab Sirr al-Khaliqa as De secretis naturae. Preserves Arabic frame narrative but does not circulate widely.

Hugo of Santalla · On the Secrets of Nature (De secretis naturae) · 1145–1151
1200s CE

TRANSLATION Philip of Tripoli translates full Secretum Secretorum

Complete Latin translation becomes one of the most widely read books of the High Middle Ages due to Aristotle's authority.

Philip of Tripoli · Secret of Secrets (Secretum Secretorum) · 1243
1300s CE

COMMENTARY Hortulanus writes commentary on Emerald Tablet

The most authoritative medieval exegesis, interpreting the Tablet as a laboratory manual for the Philosopher's Stone.

Hortulanus · Hortulanus Commentary (Liber super textum Hermetis) · 1310
1400s CE

TRANSLATION Ficino translates Corpus Hermeticum into Latin

Commissioned by Cosimo de' Medici. Ficino prioritized translating Hermes over Plato, reflecting the believed antiquity of the texts.

Marsilio Ficino · Corpus Hermeticum · 1463

PUBLICATION Pico publishes 900 Theses

Synthesis of Hermetic, Kabbalistic, Neoplatonic, and Christian doctrines. Condemned by Pope Innocent VIII.

Giovanni Pico della Mirandola · 900 Theses (Conclusiones) · 1486
1500s CE

PUBLICATION Agrippa publishes Three Books of Occult Philosophy

Most influential early modern magical synthesis integrating Hermetic, Neoplatonic, and Kabbalistic systems.

Cornelius Agrippa · Three Books of Occult Philosophy (De occulta philosophia) · 1533
1600s CE

PUBLICATION Giordano Bruno burned at the stake

Execution for heresy in Rome. Bruno's Hermetic naturalism and pantheism were among the charges.

Giordano Bruno · 1600

PUBLICATION Khunrath publishes Amphitheatrum Sapientiae Aeternae

Expanded edition with elaborate engravings linking the Emerald Tablet to VITRIOL acrostic and spiritual introspection.

Heinrich Khunrath · Amphitheatrum Sapientiae Aeternae · 1609

SCHOLARSHIP Casaubon redates the Corpus Hermeticum

Isaac Casaubon demonstrates on linguistic and stylistic grounds that the Hermetica are post-Christian, not ancient Egyptian. Undermines prisca theologia chronology.

Isaac Casaubon · Corpus Hermeticum · 1614

PUBLICATION Michael Maier publishes Atalanta Fugiens

Emblem I depicts 'the wind carried it in its womb' from the Emerald Tablet.

Michael Maier · Atalanta Fugiens · 1618

PUBLICATION Kriegsmann publishes Phoenician rendering

Speculative rendering of the Emerald Tablet in Phoenician.

1657

TRANSLATION Isaac Newton translates and comments on Emerald Tablet

Newton painstakingly translates the Tablet and composes a detailed commentary (Keynes MS 28), linking alchemical operations to Biblical Chaos and gravitational forces.

Isaac Newton · 1680
1700s CE

SCHOLARSHIP Silvestre de Sacy identifies Apollonius connection

Demonstrates the connection between the Sirr al-Khaliqa and Apollonius of Tyana, correcting earlier misidentification as an Arabic extract of Pliny.

Silvestre de Sacy · 1799
1900s CE

SCHOLARSHIP Holmyard finds Jabirian version

Eric Holmyard discovers an early Arabic version of the Emerald Tablet in the Jabirian corpus.

Eric John Holmyard · 1923

SCHOLARSHIP Ruska publishes Tabula Smaragdina

Foundational modern philological study establishing the Arabic and Latin textual history of the Emerald Tablet.

Julius Ruska · 1926

SCHOLARSHIP Kraus analyzes Jabirian corpus and Sirr al-Khaliqa

Demonstrates collective authorship of Jabirian corpus. Identifies parallels with Nemesius and Syriac Book of Treasures.

Paul Kraus · 1942

SCHOLARSHIP Weisser publishes critical edition of Sirr al-Khaliqa

Aleppo edition based on 17 manuscripts, establishing Recension A and B. Demonstrates the Tablet's original cosmological context.

Ursula Weisser · 1979

SCHOLARSHIP Fowden publishes The Egyptian Hermes

Establishes the Hellenized Egyptian priestly context for Hermetic texts.

Garth Fowden · 1986

SCHOLARSHIP Copenhaver publishes Hermetica translation

Standard English translation of Corpus Hermeticum and Asclepius with notes.

Brian P. Copenhaver · 1992
2000s CE

SCHOLARSHIP Bull publishes The Tradition of Hermes Trismegistus

Reestablishes Egyptian priestly authorship argument with ritual dimensions.

Christian H. Bull · 2018
