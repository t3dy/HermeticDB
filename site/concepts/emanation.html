Emanation (Exitus a Deo) THEOLOGICAL

Neoplatonic emanation, the One, Nous

The Neoplatonic doctrine that material multiplicity cascades from a singular, indivisible source.

The Tablet states: 'As all things were from One, by mediation of a single one, so all things were born from this one reality by a single process of adaptation.' This encapsulates the Neoplatonic doctrine of emanation. A 'One Mind' (Nous) mediates, binding celestial and terrestrial realms. Alchemical practice reverses this emanation: the substance ascends from earth to heaven (sublimation) then descends (fixation), uniting superior and inferior forces.

Significance

Overlays the Aristotelian material framework with a cosmological structure that makes alchemy a spiritual practice, not just a physical one.
