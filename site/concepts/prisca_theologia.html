Prisca Theologia (Ancient Theology) PHILOSOPHICAL

perennial philosophy, philosophia perennis

The Renaissance doctrine that a single divine truth was given to humanity in antiquity and passed through a chain of sages including Hermes, Moses, Orpheus, Pythagoras, and Plato.

Ficino and Pico positioned Hermes Trismegistus as the first link in a golden chain of ancient wisdom (prisca theologia) predating Moses and anticipating Christianity. This doctrine justified the study of pagan Hermetic texts within a Christian framework. Casaubon's 1614 redating of the Hermetica undermined the chronological claim but did not kill the idea.

Significance

The intellectual framework that made Renaissance Hermeticism possible. Without prisca theologia, studying pagan Egyptian texts would have been heresy rather than piety.
