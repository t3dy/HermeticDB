Magnum Opus (Great Work) ALCHEMICAL

Great Work, opus magnum

The alchemical process of creating the Philosopher's Stone through stages of transformation (nigredo, albedo, citrinitas, rubedo).

The Great Work proceeds through symbolic stages: nigredo (blackening/putrefaction/death), albedo (whitening/purification), citrinitas (yellowing), and rubedo (reddening/completion). Each stage corresponds to both a material chemical operation and a psychological/spiritual transformation. Hortulanus decoded the Emerald Tablet as encoding this sequence.

Significance

Central organizing metaphor of alchemical practice. Provides the interpretive framework through which the Emerald Tablet was read as a practical manual from the 14th century onward.
