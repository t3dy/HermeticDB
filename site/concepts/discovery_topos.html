Discovery Topos (Hidden Chamber Narrative) PHILOSOPHICAL

revelation narrative, pseudo-revelatory frame

The late antique literary motif of discovering ancient wisdom in a hidden underground chamber, used to authenticate esoteric knowledge.

The Emerald Tablet is introduced through a pseudo-revelatory account in which Balinas discovers a hidden chamber containing both a book and a tablet attributed to Hermes. This combines two motifs: finding a buried text and receiving a celestial inscription. The topos functions as a legitimizing device for the text's authority.

Significance

The frame narrative is essential for understanding how the Tablet was received: not as a free-floating oracle but as a discovered artifact with claimed ancient provenance.
