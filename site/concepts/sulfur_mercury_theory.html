Sulfur-Mercury Theory ALCHEMICAL

chemical wedding, Sol and Luna

The doctrine that all metals are composed of philosophical sulfur (active masculine principle) and philosophical mercury (passive feminine principle).

Hortulanus decoded 'The Sun is its father, the Moon its mother' as the chemical wedding of Philosophers' Gold (Sol, active seed) and Philosophers' Silver (Luna, passive receptacle). 'The wind carried it in its womb' refers to conception within the sealed retort's vaporous atmosphere. 'The earth is its nurse' dictates recombination with the solid ferment for stability.

Significance

The dominant interpretive lens through which the Emerald Tablet was read as practical laboratory instruction from the 14th century onward.
