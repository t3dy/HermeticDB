Telesmi / Telesmum LINGUISTIC

telesmus, ṭilasm, talisman, perfection (Newton)

Transliterated Arabic term (abu al-tilasmat, 'father of talismans') that entered Latin untranslated and became one of the most disputed words in alchemical exegesis.

The Arabic text describes the Tablet's subject as 'abu al-tilasmat' (father of talismans). Early Latin translators like Plato of Tivoli simply transliterated this as 'telesmi', retaining a link to talismanic magic. Newton, lacking Arabic context, replaced it with 'perfection'. The word's opacity in Latin generated centuries of interpretive controversy.

Significance

A key example of how the Latin tradition actively created new interpretive problems through transliteration rather than translation.
