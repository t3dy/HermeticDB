Macrocosm and Microcosm COSMOLOGICAL

as above so below, correspondence

The principle that the structure of the human being (microcosm) mirrors the structure of the universe (macrocosm).

The foundational axiom of Hermetic philosophy, formalized in the Emerald Tablet as 'That which is above is like to that which is below.' Unlike Gnostic systems that viewed the material world as flawed, Hermetic philosophy embraces the visible world as a divine reflection of the creator. Operations on the microcosmic scale (laboratory alchemy) participate in macrocosmic divine creation.

Significance

The Tablet's most famous and enduring axiom. Derives from Nemesius of Emesa's anthropology via late antique Neoplatonism.
