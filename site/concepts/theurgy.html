Theurgy (Divine Work) THEOLOGICAL

θεουργία, sacred ritual

Sacred ritual designed to enact union with the divine through participatory action rather than coercive manipulation.

Distinguished from magic (which coerces impersonal forces), theurgy is participatory and requires right intention and inner transformation. Practiced by Iamblichus and transmitted through Hermetic-priestly traditions. Components include ineffable names, synthemata (sacred symbols), ritual timing, and immaterial offerings.

Significance

Bridge between philosophical and operative Hermeticism. Iamblichus's defense of theurgy in De mysteriis is the key text linking Hermetic ritual to Neoplatonic metaphysics.
