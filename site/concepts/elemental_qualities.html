Four Elemental Qualities PHILOSOPHICAL

hot/cold/moist/dry, Aristotelian physics, hylomorphism

The four primary qualities (hot, cold, moist, dry) that combine to form the four elements and drive all material transformations.

The Sirr al-Khaliqa grounds all natural phenomena in the theory of four primary qualities emerging from primordial homogeneous matter. Combinations produce four elements (fire, air, water, earth). All properties of substances and processes are derived deductively through interactions and proportions of these qualities. This is the Aristotelian substrate of the Emerald Tablet's alchemical operations.

Significance

Provides the theoretical framework within which the Tablet's instructions for separation and recombination become intelligible.
