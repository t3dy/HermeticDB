Barbarous Names (Onomata Barbara) LINGUISTIC

sacred language, ὀνόματα βαρβαρικά

Foreign, non-Greek sacred names carrying inherent divine energy that cannot survive translation.

Iamblichus (De mysteriis 7.4-5) defends the use of Egyptian and other non-Greek sacred names in ritual, arguing that they are joined to the nature of divine beings and lose their potency when translated into Greek. The shared vocabulary of exitalos (fading) between CH XVI and De mysteriis demonstrates this theory bridges Hermetic and Neoplatonic traditions.

Significance

Foundational to theurgic practice. Embodies a theory of ritual language as energetic and ontological rather than merely conventional.
