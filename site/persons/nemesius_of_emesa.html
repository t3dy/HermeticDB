Nemesius of Emesa PHILOSOPHER

late 4th c. CE · PHILOSOPHER

Also known as: Nemesius Emesenus

Christian bishop who composed De Natura Hominis, defining the human being as a microcosm. His concept of macrocosm-microcosm correspondence is the theoretical precursor to the Emerald Tablet's central axiom.

Associated Texts

On the Nature of Man (De Natura Hominis) AUTHOR

Key Events

390 — Nemesius of Emesa writes De Natura Hominis
Defines the human being as a microcosm binding celestial and terrestrial spheres. Direct theoretical precursor to the Emerald Tablet's central axiom.
